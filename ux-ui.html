TREK: NYU Hackathon

Along with a team of three other NYU students, I spent 48 hours developing and helping execute an application that fell under the category of well-being at the 2023 Hackathon hosted by NYU. Our team came up with TREK: a mobile app that matched university students walking to a destination with another student walking in a similar direction to provide a walking partner for safety. The application has the user type in their location (input) and provides a match of a student which provides their name, university, and rating (output). The app ranks users by longest common path, keeping track of searching users in the realtime database. If both users accept, the Google Maps API will show walking or transit directions to a meeting spot, and then for both users to their destinations. Certain filters, saftey features, and authentications were considered.

I designed the intended look of the application, the logo, and assisted with the programming portion. For in depth details refer the the presentation linked here :).

Software/Tools used: Google Maps API, Firebase Realtime Database, React Native, Adobe Illustrator, and Figma
